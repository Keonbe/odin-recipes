Filipino Menudo

This rich Pinoy pork menudo dish is usually prepared on special occasions or when you're expecting company.

Menudo Ingredients:

  • 2 ¼ boneless pork chops
  • ½ pound pork liver
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 (15.5 ounce) can garbanzo beans, drained
  • 1 (1.5 ounce) box raisins
  • 2 potatoes, diced

How to Make Menudo Step-By-Step

Here's a very brief overview of what you can expect when you make Filipino Menudo (Pork and Liver Stew):

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil; add pork chops and return to a boil. Cook pork chops at a boil for 5 minutes, remove, and set aside to cool.
  2. Remove 1 cup of broth from the pot and set aside for later use. Once chops are cooled to the touch, cut them into bite-size pieces.
  3. Return water to a boil; add pork liver to the pot and cook at a boil until tender, 7 to 10 minutes. Drain and discard liquid.
  4. Set liver aside to cool; cut into bite-size pieces.
  5. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat; cook and stir onion and garlic in hot oil until tender, about 5 minutes. Stir pork chops into onion and garlic; cook and stir together for 5 minutes.
  6. Season with salt and pepper. Add tomatoes and reserved broth; cover and cook for 10 minutes. Stir pork liver, garbanzo beans, raisins, and potatoes into the mixture; cover and simmer until potatoes are fork tender, about 10 minutes.
